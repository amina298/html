Quick Facts

  • Born: October 6, 1919 (Shilavo region)
  • Served as Somalia’s head of state for 21 years
  • Led the Supreme Revolutionary Council after the 1969 coup
  • Promoted policies of "Scientific Socialism"

Timeline

  1. 1969 — Took power following a coup after the assassination of President Abdirashid Ali Sharmarke.
  2. 1974 — Became Chairman of the Organization of African Unity (OAU).
  3. 1977–1978 — Led Somalia during the Ogaden War.
  4. 1980s — Faced increasing opposition and internal conflicts.
  5. 1991 — Government collapsed; Barre fled the country.
  6. 1995 — Died in Lagos, Nigeria.

Legacy & Influence

  • Oversaw major modernization efforts and literacy campaigns.
  • His rule remains one of the most debated periods in Somali history.
  • Policies and conflicts during his era shaped Somalia's political landscape for decades.
